Bioinfomatics Notes

Molecular Biology Primer

Heredity (遗传) and DNA

  • Heredity: children resemble parents
  • Easy to see
  • Hard to explain
  • 20th century milestone: DNA discovered as the physical (molecular) carrier of hereditary informayion

Life, Cells, Proteins

  • Ths study of life = the study of cells
  • Cells are born, do their job, duplicate, die
  • All these processes controlled by molecules called proteins

Protein functions

  • “Enzymes” 酶(catalysts) 催化剂
  • Control chemical reactions in cell
  • Aspirin inhibits an enzyme that produces the “inflammation messenger”
  • Transfer of signals/molecules between and inside cells
  • sensing of environment
  • Regulate/control production of other proteins

DNA

  • DNA is a molecule: deoxyribonucleic acid 脱氧核糖核酸
  • Double helical (螺旋形的) structure (discovered by Watson, Crick & Franklin)
  • Chromosomes (染色体) are densely coiled and packed DNA
  • Sequence of “nucleotides” (A/C/G/T) as you march through either “strand” (”Base” = nucleotides)

Protein

  • Protein is a sequence of amino-acids (氨基酸)
  • 20 possible amino acids
  • The amino-acid sequence “folds” into a 3-D structure called protein

From DNA to Protein: In words

  1. DNA = nucleotide sequence
  • Alphabet size = 4 (A,C,G,T)
  1. DNA → mRNA (single stranded)
  • Alphabet size = 4 (A,C,G,U)
  1. mRNA → amino acid sequence
  • Alphabet size = 20
  1. Amino acid sequence “folds” into 3-dimensional molecule called protein

RNA

  • RNA = ribonucleic acid
  • “U” instead of “T”
  • Usually single stranded
  • Has base-pairing capability
  • Can form simple non-linear structures
  • Life may have started with RNA

DNA and Genes

  • DNA is a very “long” molecule
  • If kept straight, will cover 5cm (!!) in human cell
  • DNA in human has 3 billion base-pairs
  • String of 3 billion characters
  • DNA harbors “genes”
  • A gene is a substring of the DNA string
  • A gene “codes” for a protein

Genes code for proteins

  • DNA → mRNA → protein can actually be written as Gene → mRNA → protein
  • A gene is typically few hundred base-pairs (bp) long

Transcription (DNA→mRNA)

  • Process of making a single stranded mRNA using double stranded DNA as template
  • Only genes are transctibed, not all DNA
  • Gene has a transcription “start site” and a transcription “stop site”

Translation (mRNA→Amino acid sequence)

  • Process of making an amino acid sequence from (single stranded) mRNA
  • Each triplet of bases translates into one amino acid
  • Each such triplet is called “codon” 密码子
  • The translation is basically a table lookup

Gene structure

  • Exons and Introns
  • Introns are “spliced” out, and are not part of mRNA
  • Promoter (upstream) of gene

Gene expression

  • Process of making a protein from a gene as template
  • Transcription, then translation
  • Can be regulated

Gene Regulation

  • Chromosomal (染色体的) activation/ deactivation
  • Transcriptional regulation
  • Slicing regulation
  • mRNA degradation
  • mRNA transport regulation
  • Control of translation initiation
  • Post-translational modification

Genome 基因组

  • The entire sequence of DNA in a cell
  • All cells have the same genome
  • All cells came from repeated duplications starting from initial cell (zygote)
  • Human genome is 99.9% identical among individuals
  • Human genome is 3 billion base-pairs (bp) long

Genome features

  • Genes
  • Regulatory sequences
  • The above two make up 5% of human genome
  • What’s the rest doing? (we don’t know)
  • “Annotating” the genome (task of bioinfomatics)

Epigentics (实验胚胎学): decorations on the genome

  • Decorations are “condition-dependent”: can vary from one cell to another
  • Most DNA is wrapped around a “histone core”, to form nucleosomes
  • Chromatin: compacted DNA
  • DNA methylation: methyl (CH3) added to some of the ‘C’s in DNA
  • Dnase I hypersensitive sites: DNA segments between nucleosomes

Histone (组蛋白) and modifications

  • Closer look at “DNA wrapped around histone core”.
  • Histone is a “complex” of multiple proteins. Those proteins have “tails”
  • Modifications of the histone tails can “loosen” the interaction with DNA

Evolution

  • A model/theory to explain the diversity of life forms
  • Some aspects known, some not
  • Mutation, selection, fitness
  • An active field of research in itself
  • Bioinformatics deals with genomes, which are end-products of evolution. Hence bioinformatics cannot ignore the study of evolution
  • All organisms share the genetic code
  • Similar genes across species
  • Probably had a common ancestor
  • Genomes are a wonderful resource to trace back the history of life
  • Got to be careful though – the inferences may require clever techniques
